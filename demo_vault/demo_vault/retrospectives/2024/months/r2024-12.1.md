The initial positive shift was largely undone, suggesting a cycle where external stressors quickly depleted any gains in resilience, leading back to a state of high stress, emotional agitation, and a profound sense of exhaustion and "wretchedness."

Furthermore, an implicit understanding of the necessity for emotional regulation emerged, evident in conscious attempts to withdraw from escalating conflicts. The author also reflected on the "folly of judging of others by ourselves," acknowledging internal struggles and a sense of self-blame regarding their current "difficult situation," suggesting a nascent understanding of their own agency and perceived lack thereof.
